What is a recipe?

A "bioinformatics recipe" is a collection of commands used to perform a bioinformatics data analysis task. A recipe can be thought of as a bioinformatics workflow or a bioinformatics pipeline.

Where are the recipes?

  • The Project List page contains recipes collected into projects.
  • The Latest Recipes page displays the most recently updated recipes.

What types of recipes can I find here?

Most recipes demonstrate typical use cases for various bioinformatics tools. Many recipes have been developed to support bioinformatics education.

Can I run recipes on my computer?

Yes! All recipes are designed to run both through this website and at the command line on your computer.

Where can I learn more?

Scientific publication: Bioinformatics recipes: creating, executing and distributing reproducible data analysis workflows by Natay Aberra, Aswathy Sebastian, Aaron P. Maloy, Christopher B. Rees, Meredith L. Bartron, and Istvan Albert , BMC Bioinformatics, (2020) .
